What is DMIT?

DMIT is based on the scientific study of fingerprints and their correlation with multiple intelligence and learning styles.

Scientific Foundation

Dermatoglyphics is the scientific study of ridged skin patterns found on fingers, palms, toes and soles. These patterns are formed during the 13th to 19th week of gestation and remain unchanged throughout life.

Research has established a correlation between fingerprint patterns and brain development, as both are formed during the same period in fetal development.

Genetic Basis: Based on genetic coding
Brain Correlation: Linked to brain development
Permanent: Patterns never change
Unique: Individual patterns
